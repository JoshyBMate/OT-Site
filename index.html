As an Occupational Therapist I believe that participation in daily life can be supported or restricted by physical, affective or cognitive abilities of the individual, the characteristics of the occupation or the physical, social, cultural, attitudinal and legislative environments. Therefore, this occupational therapy practice is focused on enabling individuals to change aspects of their person, the occupation, the environment or some combination of the enhance occupational participation.The clients are actively involved in the occupational process. The therapy is client-driven and diverse and measured in terms of participation, satisfaction derived from occupational participation and / or improvement in occupational performance.

Practice goal

This practice’s primary goal is to enable people to participate in activities of everyday life. This outcome is achieved by working with all age groups. I help them to engage in the occupations they want, need to or are expected to do, or by modifying the occupation or the environment to better support their occupational engagement.

My education and experience

As an Occupational Therapist, I, have a broad education in the medical, social behavioural, psychological, psychosocial and occupational sciences which equips me with the attitude, skill and knowledge to work collaboratively with all age groups, individually or in groups or communities.

Definition of Occupational Therapy

Occupational Therapy is a client-centred health profession concerned with promoting health and wellbeing through occupation. Occupational Therapy is a health profession and require specific university level education.

Specializations

I specialise in, individual school related developmental delays, individual behaviour modification, life skills and emotional awereness training ranging from children to adults. As well as, life skills group training for coroprate enviroments.
